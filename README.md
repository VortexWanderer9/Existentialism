# Existentialism: Seeing Life Without Illusion

Existentialism is the idea that human beings are born into a world without automatic meaning, and because of that, each person must create meaning through the way they live.

It begins when someone starts questioning everything around them.

Why do people chase status so much?  
Why do people pretend to be important?  
Why do many people act differently depending on who they are with?  
Why does society feel like a performance sometimes?

Existentialism says most people live by following roles created by society:
- worker
- rich person
- successful person
- powerful leader
- respected citizen

People become trapped inside these identities and forget who they really are.

A person may spend their whole life trying to “look valuable” instead of becoming truly honest with themselves.

That is why the world can start feeling fake when you observe it deeply.

You begin noticing:
- people acting instead of being real
- fake confidence
- fake superiority
- social competition
- people wanting to feel above others

Existentialism does not say life is meaningless in a negative way.

It says:
> Meaning is not automatically given to us.

Humans create meaning themselves.

A title does not make someone important.  
Money does not make someone better.  
Power does not make someone wise.

At the end, every human is still human:
- everyone feels pain
- everyone wants love
- everyone fears death
- everyone wants to matter

Existentialism teaches that real freedom begins when you stop blindly following society’s expectations and start living consciously.

Not for status.  
Not for approval.  
Not to appear superior.

But because you genuinely choose your own values.

The goal is not to hate the world.  
The goal is to see reality clearly without losing your humanity.

True existence is not about becoming “higher” than others.

It is about becoming real.
